Fundamentals

Develop Explosive Power

Explosive Power refers to an individual’s ability to exert a maximal amount of force in the shortest possible time interval. An athlete’s ability to display a high level of explosive power is believed to be one of the most important factors in determining athletic success. You will develop greater power and explosion through sports specific exercises that you will execute in the weight room. Boosting your power and explosion will guarantee to have you run faster and jump higher.

Foot Speed

The Fastest Athletes have their feet spending less time on the ground. In your Peak Performance Program you will develop Greater Foot Speed by executing proven strategies to improve your ground contact time.

Quick Questions To Ask

· Are you running on the ball of your foot?
· Are you running in core neutral position?

When you run in neutral position, the power flows and you have the opportunity for greater activation and use of the large muscles of your hips and hamstrings.

· When you run do you have high knees? Are your toes pointed up? As I say with my guys over and over again. Knee Up, Toe Up, Knee Up, Toe Up, Knee Up, Toe Up.
· What is your arm position? Are your elbows at a 90-degree angle? Are your arms swinging from the shoulder from the chin through the hip?

The development of your mind to achieve Peak Performance. The Mental Training portion of your Peak Performance Program will Focus on 4 core Aspects.

Goal Setting

You need to have very clear short-term, midterm, and long-range goals. Peak Performers set out to turn their dreams into reality by taking action through goal setting... Mental athletes are goal-oriented. They have vision. When an athlete complains of lacking motivation, you can be sure that it’s almost always caused by goals that fail to inspire him to action. Goals serve to keep you on target. They increase the desire to achieve.

Visualization or Mental Rehearsal

Peak Performers must practice seeing themselves training successfully in their mind. Athletes, who visualize how to handle training situations and competition scenarios successfully tend to be far more successful in actual practice.

Self Talk

The average person thinks 50,000 thoughts a day. If the majority of that self-talk is negative then performance will be affected. The first step in optimizing self-talk is to recognize that it is there and then working on challenging the negative words and beliefs.

Breathing/Arousal Control

Becoming aware of our breathing and taking control of it when we need to is one of the most powerful ways to take control of ourselves, especially when we’re about to compete. Creating the wiring in our brain to calm ourselves in a stressful situation will help us make more effective choices, be less reactive and ultimately help us to survive the situation as best we can.
